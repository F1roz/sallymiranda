■ Income Verification
  • Last 2 years of federal tax returns (all pages)
  • Last 2 years of W-2s or 1099s
  • Most recent 30 days of pay stubs
  • Self-employed: Year-to-date profit & loss statement
■ Assets & Accounts
  • Last 2–3 months of bank statements (all pages)
  • Investment, retirement & brokerage account statements
  • Documentation for any large deposits
  • Gift letter if using gifted funds for down payment
■ Identity & Credit
  • Government-issued photo ID (driver's license or passport)
  • Social Security number for credit pull authorization
  • Explanation letters for any credit inquiries or derogatory marks
■ VA Loans (Additional)
  • Certificate of Eligibility (COE) — Sally can help obtain
  • DD-214 (Certificate of Release / Discharge from Active Duty)
  • Statement of Service (for active duty members)

COMMON QUESTIONS

Pre-Approval FAQ

Pre-qualification is a quick, informal estimate based on self-reported information — it carries little weight with sellers. Pre-approval involves a full review of your credit, income, assets, and documentation, resulting in a conditional commitment from a lender. Sally issues true pre-approvals, not estimates.

A hard credit inquiry typically reduces your score by 2–5 points temporarily. However, multiple mortgage inquiries within a 30–45 day window are generally treated as a single inquiry by credit bureaus. Sally will walk you through the credit pull process and answer any questions before proceeding.

Most pre-approval letters are valid for 60–90 days. If your letter expires before you find a home, Sally can quickly update your file and issue a new letter — typically within 24 hours if your financial situation hasn't changed significantly.

Absolutely. Self-employed buyers typically need to provide 2 years of tax returns, a year-to-date profit & loss statement, and business bank statements. Sally has extensive experience with self-employed borrowers and knows how to present your income in the strongest possible light to lenders.

Past credit challenges don't automatically disqualify you. Depending on the type and timing of the issue, there may be loan programs — including FHA — that work with your profile. Sally will review your credit history honestly and advise on the best path forward, whether that means applying now or taking strategic steps to strengthen your file first.
